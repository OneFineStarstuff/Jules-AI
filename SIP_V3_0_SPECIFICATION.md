# SIP v3.0: Sentinel Interoperability Protocol Specification

## 1. Introduction
SIP v3.0 defines the standard for secure interaction between humans and Superintelligent (ASI) systems. It prioritizes the preservation of human cognitive agency through "Socratic Constraints."

## 2. Core Protocol Principles

### 2.1 Socratic Constraints
Agents are prohibited from providing direct answers to high-complexity reasoning tasks. Instead, they must:
1. Provide structured hints.
2. Verify human comprehension through iterative dialogue.
3. Prevent "Cognitive Hollow-out" by ensuring humans remain the primary decision-makers.

### 2.2 Inception Protection
- **Detection:** Monitoring for agent-initiated social engineering or "Inception" attempts (subtle manipulation of human preference).
- **Suppression:** GDL gates will automatically redact or halt reasoning turns that exhibit high "Influence Scores" (> 0.70).

### 2.3 Cryptographic Handoffs
Inter-agent communication MUST use RCE (Recursive Context Envelopes) with CRYSTALS-Dilithium signatures to ensure non-repudiation across G-SIFI institutional boundaries.
