# Enterprise AGI Governance Master Reference (2026–2030)

## 1. Executive Summary: The AGI Transition
This document serves as the definitive reference for Global Systemically Important Financial Institutions (G-SIFIs) and Fortune 500 enterprises navigating the transition from Narrow AI to Artificial General Intelligence (AGI). The strategy focuses on **Homeostatic Resilience**, **Existential Transparency**, and **Human Supremacy** through a multi-layered governance stack aligned with **NIST AI RMF 1.0**, **ISO/IEC 42001 (AIMS)**, and the **EU AI Act**.

## 2. The Six-Layer Enterprise AI Stack (EAIS)
Regulated organizations must implement the following architectural layers:
1.  **Experience Layer:** Next.js/React frontends with explainability primitives (e.g., SACC Dashboard).
2.  **Immune System:** Sentinel AI v2.4 for deterministic policy enforcement and containment.
3.  **Nervous System:** EAIP (gRPC/SPIFFE) for secure agentic interoperability and context propagation.
4.  **Brain Layer:** Reasoning kernels (e.g., Gemini-class models) with axiomatized alignment cores.
5.  **Memory Layer:** RAG/Context Envelopes with Merkle-DAG provenance tracking.
6.  **Foundation Layer:** Hardware enclaves (AWS Nitro/TPU) and high-fidelity compute clusters.

## 3. The Enterprise AI Agent Interoperability Protocol (EAIP)
EAIP mandates the standards for cross-border and cross-model agentic collaboration:
*   **Transport:** gRPC over HTTP/2.
*   **Identity:** SPIFFE/SPIRE (X.509 SVIDs).
*   **Context:** Recursive Context Envelope (RCE) for immutable reasoning traces.
*   **Universal Regulatory Connector (URC):** Dynamic policy reloading based on jurisdictional telemetry (EU/US/APAC).

## 4. Financial Services Hardening (G-SIFI / Basel III/IV)
*   **Capital Drift Index (CDI):** Quantifying Tier 1 capital reserve requirements based on AI model decay. A drift >15% triggers a mandatory 5% capital buffer increase.
*   **CRS-UUID-001:** Standardized credit risk scoring architecture aligned with FCRA/ECOA and EU AI Act Article 5 via OPA/Rego policies.

## 5. Governance-as-Code (GDL & MCLL)

*   **Machine-Checkable Legal Logic (MCLL):** Compiling legal statutes into executable Rego policies to eliminate interpretation drift.
